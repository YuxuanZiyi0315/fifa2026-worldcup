# When and Where is the FIFA World Cup 2026? Complete Guide to Dates and Venues

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is shaping up to be the most ambitious and exciting tournament in football history. As the first World Cup to be hosted across three nations and featuring an expanded 48-team format, fans around the globe are eager to know exactly when and where this historic event will take place.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about the **FIFA World Cup 2026 dates, host cities, stadiums, and schedule**.

## When is the FIFA World Cup 2026?

The **FIFA World Cup 2026** will take place from **June 11 to July 19, 2026**.

This marks a return to the traditional June-July tournament window, following the unprecedented November-December scheduling of Qatar 2022 due to extreme summer heat concerns in the Middle East.

### Key Tournament Dates:
- **Opening Match**: June 11, 2026
- **Group Stage**: June 11 - June 30, 2026
- **Round of 32**: July 1 - July 4, 2026
- **Round of 16**: July 5 - July 8, 2026
- **Quarter-finals**: July 9 - July 10, 2026
- **Semi-finals**: July 14 - July 15, 2026
- **Third-place Match**: July 18, 2026
- **Final**: July 19, 2026

The tournament will span **39 days**, making it the longest World Cup in history – two days longer than the 2014 tournament in Brazil.

## Where is the FIFA World Cup 2026 Being Held?

The **FIFA World Cup 2026** will be hosted across **three countries**: the **United States, Canada, and Mexico**. This is the first World Cup ever to be co-hosted by three nations.

### Host Countries Overview:

| Country | Number of Host Cities | Number of Matches | Key Facts |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **United States** | 11 cities | 60 matches | Hosts quarter-finals, semi-finals, and final |
| **Mexico** | 3 cities | 10 matches | Opens tournament; most matches in Mexico City |
| **Canada** | 2 cities | 10 matches | First World Cup matches ever in Canada |

## Why Was the 2026 World Cup Awarded to North America?

FIFA selected the **United 2026 bid** (USA, Canada, Mexico) in June 2018, defeating a rival bid from Morocco. The North American bid won with **134 votes** compared to Morocco's 65 votes.

Key factors in the successful bid included:
- ✅ **Guaranteed financial success** (projected $11 billion revenue)
- ✅ **Existing world-class stadium infrastructure**
- ✅ **Proven track record** (USA 1994, Mexico 1970 & 1986)
- ✅ **Massive commercial market** for sponsors and broadcasters

## Expanded Format: 48 Teams in 2026

The **FIFA World Cup 2026** will be the first to feature **48 teams**, expanded from the 32-team format used from 1998 to 2022.

### New Tournament Structure:

**Group Stage:**
- 12 groups of 4 teams each
- Top 2 from each group + 8 best third-placed teams advance
- Total group stage matches: 72 (up from 64)

**Knockout Stage:**
- Round of 32 (new stage)
- Round of 16
- Quarter-finals
- Semi-finals
- Final

**Total Matches**: **104 matches** (up from 64 in previous tournaments)

This expanded format means **more matches, more cities, and a longer tournament** – hence the importance of knowing exactly when and where each match takes place.

The tournament starts on **June 11, 2026** and ends on **July 19, 2026** with the final.

The final will be played at **MetLife Stadium** in East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York City area).

### Will matches be played in all three countries?
Yes, matches will be played across **16 cities in the USA (11), Mexico (3), and Canada (2)**.
