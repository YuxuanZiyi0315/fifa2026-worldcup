In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore **every stadium hosting World Cup 2026 matches**, including capacity, location, history, and which matches they'll host.

## Most Important Stadiums for World Cup 2026

### 1. MetLife Stadium - The Final Venue 🏆

**Location**: East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York City area)  
**Capacity**: 82,500 (largest for World Cup 2026)  
**Matches**: 8 total, including the **Final on July 19, 2026**

MetLife Stadium will host the most prestigious match in world football – the **FIFA World Cup 2026 Final**. Located just 5 miles from Manhattan, this stadium offers easy access to one of the world's most famous cities.

**Key Facts:**
- Opened: 2010
- Home team: New York Jets and New York Giants (NFL)
- Roof: Open-air (no roof)
- Notable: Hosted Super Bowl XLVIII (2014)

### 2. SoFi Stadium - The Opening Match 🎪

**Location**: Inglewood, California (Los Angeles area)  
**Capacity**: 70,240  
**Matches**: 8 total, including the **Opening Match on June 11, 2026**

SoFi Stadium is one of the most technologically advanced stadiums in the world, featuring a massive video screen and a futuristic canopy roof. It's a strong candidate to host the opening match.

**Key Facts:**
- Opened: 2020
- Construction cost: $5 billion (most expensive stadium ever built)
- Roof: Fixed canopy (covers 70% of seats)
- Notable: Hosted Super Bowl LVI (2022)

### 3. AT&T Stadium - Most Matches 🏟️

**Location**: Arlington, Texas (Dallas-Fort Worth area)  
**Capacity**: 80,000 (expandable to 100,000)  
**Matches**: 9 total (most of any venue)

Known as "Jerry World" (after Dallas Cowboys owner Jerry Jones), AT&T Stadium will host the most matches of any venue in World Cup 2026. Its retractable roof makes it weather-proof.

**Key Facts:**
- Opened: 2009
- Home team: Dallas Cowboys (NFL)
- Roof: Retractable
- Notable: Houses the world's largest video screen (160 feet wide)

### 4. Estadio Azteca - Historic Third World Cup 🌎

**Location**: Mexico City, Mexico  
**Capacity**: 87,523 (largest in North America)  
**Matches**: 5 total, including **Opening Ceremony**

The Estadio Azteca is making history as the **first stadium ever to host three FIFA World Cups** (1970, 1986, and 2026). Its high altitude (7,200 feet above sea level) makes it a unique challenge for players.

**Key Facts:**
- Opened: 1966
- Historic matches: 1970 Final (Brazil vs Italy), 1986 Final (Argentina vs West Germany)
- Altitude: 2,200 meters (7,200 feet)
- Notable: Diego Maradona's "Goal of the Century" and "Hand of God" goals scored here in 1986

## Stadium Selection Process: How Were Venues Chosen?

FIFA and the United 2026 Bid Committee used rigorous criteria to select host stadiums:

### Selection Criteria:
1. ✅ **Minimum capacity**: 40,000 for group stage; 60,000+ for semifinals and final
2. ✅ **Infrastructure**: Adequate parking, public transportation, and fan facilities
3. ✅ **Location**: Geographic diversity across host countries
4. ✅ **Climate**: Consideration of weather conditions (roof or indoor preferred for summer heat)
5. ✅ **Existing use**: Stadiums must be operational and hosting major events

### Stadiums That Were Considered But Not Selected:
- **Washington D.C.** (FedExField) - Removed due to stadium conditions
- **Baltimore** (M&T Bank Stadium) - Not selected
- **Edmonton** (Commonwealth Stadium) - Removed from Canadian bid
- **Chicago** - Withdrew from consideration

## Stadium Features and Fan Experience

### Climate Control and Roof Coverage

With the World Cup scheduled for **June-July** (summer in North America), heat will be a major concern. Fortunately, **9 out of 16 stadiums** have either retractable roofs or fixed roofs:

**Stadiums with Roofs:**
1. SoFi Stadium (fixed canopy)
2. AT&T Stadium (retractable)
3. Mercedes-Benz Stadium (retractable)
4. NRG Stadium (retractable)
5. BC Place (retractable)
6. Allegiant Stadium (fixed dome) - *Not selected*
7. Hard Rock Stadium (retractable in planning)

**Open-Air Stadiums (weather-dependent):**

- Arrowhead Stadium
- Lincoln Financial Field

- Gillette Stadium

- Estadio Akron

### Grass Quality and Field Conditions

All World Cup matches must be played on **natural grass**. Several NFL stadiums normally use artificial turf and will need to install temporary grass surfaces:

**Stadiums Requiring Temporary Grass:**
- MetLife Stadium ✅
- SoFi Stadium ✅ (already has grass)
- AT&T Stadium ✅ (has grass)
- Mercedes-Benz Stadium ✅ (has grass)
- Hard Rock Stadium ✅ (has grass)
- Levi's Stadium ✅ (has grass)

FIFA has strict requirements for field quality, and each stadium will have its playing surface inspected and certified before the tournament.
